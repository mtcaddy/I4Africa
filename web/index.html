About Us

We are a group of international formation of individuals, who are social entrepreneurs, change makers and stubborn optimists. While on a day to day, work across a vast range of industries and organizations, devote time, energy and creativity towards mapping solutions that can help the most vulnerable during the fight against COVID-19.
Decentrally organized - Locally applied

Mission

Unify knowledge, skills and resources that can help low income and vulnerable local communities in Africa to be self-sufficient with quick responsive and responsible action to protect and surpass the curve of the COVID19 spread.

Vision

Every individual has better access to the resources - water, masks and hand hygiene - needed to keep safe and healthy.

Why we are doing it

While every part of the world is acting towards flattening the curve of the spread of COVID-19, there are major groups of society that are more prone and exposed to its effects. These effects are medical, social and economical. The societies of the developing countries, have the largest percentage of individuals who do not have adequate access to

  • WATER: the number one preventive measure for avoiding contamination of the virus
  • HOUSING: Living poor housing conditions, making social distancing impossible.
  • FOOD: Living day to day. Unable to access the basic needs of nutrition due to lockdowns, curfews and closed food markets, in addition to increasing food prices.
  • MEDICAL CARE: insufficient medical care system, lack of medical personnel, equipment, unreliable electricity are a norm in many of the countries.
All of this was happening before COVID-19. Now with the quick deadly spread of the virus, showing especially critical symptoms and deadly results to those with poor health history, these puts those with the above circumstance at the MOST vulnerable.

What we are doing
      Identifying local actors through a local verification process of ability to deliver, adequate environment for production and ability to deliver and distribute the products to the community.
    1. Mobilizing unconventional local actors that can fill the gaps in producing personal protective equipment (PPE) for the most vulnerable communities. These adaptive measures utilize the resources available locally and are unique to the country and in some cases the community.
    2. Providing clear consistent blueprint to makers to ensure quality and consistency of face masks and hand hygiene. As well as strengthening local awareness by communicating the why and how use of the masks and hygiene measures according to WHO guidelines.
    3. Create connected communication channels between production and demand.
By learning from pilots with communities in Nigeria and Uganda, the project will expand to other countries as the crisis continues, focused on providing the know-how of local face masks production and hand hygiene needs.
